01
Problem statement

The SBL collateral verification step is the highest-risk, highest-effort step in the underwriting pipeline. Three structural problems make it unsustainable at scale.

Risk detection
Inconsistent. Human-dependent.
Third-party pledge mismatches are caught only if the underwriter notices them. No systematic check exists. Two reviewers on the same case can reach different conclusions.
Cognitive load
~63 min of mechanics per case.
Document cold-read (~30 min), advisor email drafting (~15 min), policy lookup (~18 min). None of it requires expert judgment. All of it consumes expert time.
Auditability
No AI decision trail.
As regulators begin requiring documented AI decision trails, the current workflow logs nothing. Every AI-influenced step is invisible to compliance.

Hypothesis: If Aria absorbs the mechanics, detects risks systematically, and logs every step — underwriters catch more, close faster, and produce a compliance record that scales. We’ll know within 90 days.

03
User personas

Two internal users. Different experience levels. Different failure modes. One product must serve both.

WC
Mid-level · Primary target
William Chen
SBL Underwriter · 3 years · 8–15 cases/week
“Make a confident, defensible decision on every case without missing a risk signal.”
Pain points
  • No systematic flag for third-party pledge mismatches — relies on noticing the discrepancy while reading.
  • Writes every advisor email from scratch. 15 min of logistics that requires knowledge but not judgment.
  • Policy questions pull him out of the case. Each interruption costs 12–18 min of recovery time.
  • Won’t trust AI that just gives answers. Needs the source to confirm. “Aria says so” isn’t compliance-defensible.
Aria must
  • Surface risks he’d miss — with the source cited every time.
  • Eliminate the drafting and lookup work. Free him for judgment calls.
  • Feel like a faster version of his own process, not a junior checking his work.
MR
Senior · 10+ years
Margaret Reyes
SBL Senior Underwriter · 10+ years · Team quality standard
“Make me faster. Don’t make me prove I already know how to do my job.”
Pain points
  • Rigid checklists feel condescending. A tool that walks her through steps she’s done 500 times gets ignored.
  • Opaque AI destroys trust fast. One confidently wrong answer with no reasoning and she’s done with it.
  • Higher volume = mechanical overhead costs her more total time than it costs William.
  • High adoption risk: if she publicly dismisses Aria, the whole team follows her lead.
Aria must
  • Get out of her way. Collapsible panel, minimal friction, no mandatory hand-holding.
  • Surface the 5% of cases she hasn’t seen before — without crying wolf on the 95% she knows cold.
  • Earn her endorsement. Her “Aria is useful” is worth more than any onboarding campaign.
The core design tension
“William needs structure to stay safe. Margaret needs freedom to stay fast. The solution: structured default, expert compression — same AI intelligence, different friction levels.”

07
Product trade-offs

Six deliberate choices. What we chose, what we gave up, and why the trade was worth it.

DecisionWhat we chose & whyWhat we gave up
Linear checklist
Structured
 Creates an auditable step-by-step record. Ensures junior underwriters can’t skip risk flags. Compliance requires it. Expert freedom. Senior underwriters may resent the sequence. Mitigated by the Aria panel alongside.
Human confirms every task
Human in loop
 Trust must be earned before delegation. We need 90 days of override data before automating any step. A single high-profile auto-approval error sets the AI program back 18 months. Maximum throughput. Confirmation adds friction. We design Confirm to be <10 seconds to minimize it.
Aria panel is opt-in
Collapsible
 Senior underwriters reject tools that feel imposed. Opt-in signals the AI is working for them, not supervising them. Always-on guidance. Mitigated by proactive inline banners on task cards for high-confidence findings.
Email draft is inline
No context switch
 Every tool switch costs 23 minutes of cognitive recovery. Draft stays inside the workflow. Underwriter reviews and sends without leaving the case. Email client preference. Copy-to-clipboard available as a fallback.
“Needs Review” not “Revoke”
Label
 “Revoke” makes overrides feel punitive. Underwriters suppress them. We need overrides — they are our most important quality signal. The label protects the data. One fewer word. Worth it.
Always show source
Cited always
 Uncited AI answers are not compliance-defensible in SBL. Citations let underwriters verify reasoning and give them language to defend decisions. Opaque AI is a liability, not a feature. Visual simplicity. Managed with progressive disclosure: summary visible, full citation on expand.
08
AI design guidelines

Six principles specific to SBL’s regulated, high-accountability context.

01
Recommend, don’t decide
Every Aria output is a recommendation. The underwriter’s Confirm is the decision. This is the product’s legal posture and its trust model.
Right: “I recommend confirming ownership.”  Wrong: “Ownership: Confirmed.”
02
Always cite the source
Every finding references the document, page, and field. An uncited AI answer is not defensible in a compliance review.
In practice: “Fidelity statement p.5 — account I83894899 registered to Powell Family Trust.”
03
Design uncertainty first
The cases that break Aria are the highest-stakes ones. Low-confidence and escalation states are not afterthoughts — they are the most important design work.
In practice: Amber badge + “Outside my confidence range — recommend escalating.” One-tap path always visible.
04
Reduce cognitive load
If adding an AI feature requires more clicks or more reading, it doesn’t ship. Every feature is measured by decisions removed, not capabilities added.
In practice: Chip prompts give a direct answer in one tap. No panel, no typing, no waiting.
05
Feedback is a product feature
The override delta — what Aria said vs. what the underwriter did — is the training signal that compounds model quality each quarter.
In practice: “Needs Review” triggers “What would be more accurate?” — pre-set options + free text. 15 seconds max.
06
Log everything. Keep it reversible.
Every AI output, every confirmation, every timestamp — logged. Every confirmed step re-openable. The activity feed is the product’s compliance backbone.
In practice: “12:04 Aria flagged pledge risk → 12:07 William confirmed → 12:09 outreach sent.”
09
AI UX design patterns

Five interaction patterns that make Aria feel like part of the workflow, not a separate tool bolted on.

AI Summary Card
Proactive briefing
Aria speaks first before the underwriter takes any action. Delivers ranked risks and a linked checklist. Eliminates the 25 min cold-read.
Problem
25 min cold-read replaced by 2 min structured brief.
Model
AI acts first. Human reviews and confirms.
→ Step 5 entry · collateral verification
Confirm / Needs Review
Human-in-loop checkpoint
Every AI task has a binary human gate. Confirm advances the workflow. Needs Review flags it for correction. The label choice protects the override rate signal.
Problem
Audit gaps when AI outputs go unconfirmed.
Model
AI acts. Human gates. No task advances without a decision.
→ All 4 checklist task cards
Inline Chip Prompts
Contextual micro-entry
Task-specific action buttons embedded inside each card. “Explain this risk” or “Rewrite with Aria” fires a pre-scoped query in one tap — no panel required.
Problem
Opening the full panel for a predictable question adds unnecessary friction.
Model
1 tap → answer inline. No panel state.
→ Pledge risk, outreach, and verification cards
Collapsible AI Panel
Opt-in deep engagement
Full Aria chat in a sliding right panel, opened on demand. Never forced. Respects expert autonomy — the single most important adoption signal for senior underwriters.
Problem
Forced AI panels feel like supervision. Senior users reject them.
Model
Human-initiated. AI-responsive. Panel preserves workspace context.
→ Global · accessible from any step
AI Document Preview
Extraction + source verification
Split-pane modal: raw document on the left, Aria’s extracted fields on the right. Clicking any extraction row highlights the source passage in the document and jumps to the correct page. Verification becomes a 3-second action per field — not a manual re-read.
Problem
Underwriters had to hunt source text manually to trust Aria’s extractions — defeating the time savings.
Model
AI extracts first. Human clicks any field. Document auto-scrolls and highlights.
→ Ownership card, pledgor verification, document collection — any step requiring document-level review
The pattern principle
“Aria acts. The human gates. The record reflects a human decision. That sequence is both the right compliance posture and the only way to build trust that survives the first time Aria is wrong.”
